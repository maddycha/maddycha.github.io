overview

Abbi is a conversational user interface designed to help streamline the process of checking in and out of an Airbnb.

To accomplish this, Abbi can provide specific check-in and check-out information, contact hosts, recommending restaurants/experiences.

problem

A lack of a universal check-in process is a cause of frustration for both guests and hosts

motion studies

We started off the process by creating animations based on Cortana states

We worked with similar restraints individually and then came together to compare how we represented each emotion.

solutioning

From here, we began to consider what features we would help implement within the current ecosystem

We began to analyze the current user journey, comparing user testimonies and pain points. As mentioned, there is no universal check-in process—hosts often relied on sending outside resources such as through Google Docs, PDFs, emails, plain text, etc. To help remedy this issue, we decided to create an easy way of consolidating this information in a way that would be easily accessible to people on-the-go.

defining abbi

Abbi's design stems from the round/friendly shapes of brand's design language and travel aspect of airbnb

We designed abbi to supplement text and images inputted by hosts, therefore we did not want the graphic or animation to be too conspicuous. As the existing application is the main way for users to receive information about their airbnb while on the go, we decided that abbi would reside within the app, and designed quick screens as a proof of concept.

storyboarding

For our concept video, we decided to focus on the guest check-in experience

Our biggest challenge was to highlight parts of the application screens simultaneously with the character animation without making the flow of the video too overwhelming.

assets

We created assets based on the Airbnb illustration guidelines

We focused a lot of our time designing our main persona as they would be highlighted most throughout the video. From there, we created backgrounds and various assets that would visually support the protagonist without being too overwhelming or competing.

final

Once all the assets were finalized, we used After Effects to put everything together
